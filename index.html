The Logic Gates

AND Gate

The AND gate outputs 1 (TRUE) only when both of its inputs are 1. If either input is 0, the output is 0.

Think of it like two switches in series — both must be on for the light to work.

OR Gate

The OR gate outputs 1 (TRUE) when at least one of its inputs is 1. It only outputs 0 when both inputs are 0.

Think of it like two switches in parallel — either one being on will turn the light on.

NOT Gate

The NOT gate (also called an inverter) has only one input. It outputs the opposite of whatever it receives — 1 becomes 0, and 0 becomes 1.

The small circle at the output is the symbol for inversion.

Connecting Logic Gates

Step 1 — Start from an Input

The lettered boxes on the left side of the canvas (A, B, C…) are your inputs. They represent signals going into your circuit.

To start a connection, hover over the dot on the right side of an input, then click and drag from it towards a gate.

Step 2 — Connect to a Gate's Input

Each gate has input anchors on its left side — one per input line. Drag your wire onto one of these to make a connection.

A blue dot will appear when your wire snaps to a valid connection point. You can connect the same input to multiple gates.

Step 3 — Chain Gates Together

The output of one gate (the line on its right) can be connected to the input of another gate. This is how you build complex circuits.

For example, to build Q = (A AND B) OR C, connect A and B into an AND gate, then connect the AND gate's output and C into an OR gate.

Step 4 — Connect to the Output Q

The Q box on the right side of the canvas is your output. Your final gate's output must be connected to Q to complete the circuit.

Once Q is connected, press Submit to check if your circuit matches the expression shown at the top.

Tip — Removing Gates

Made a mistake? Drag any gate onto the bin in the bottom-right corner of the canvas to delete it. Any wires connected to that gate will also be removed.

You can also press the Clear button to remove everything and start fresh.

Reading a Logic Expression

What is a logic expression?

A logic expression is like a maths equation, but instead of adding numbers, you're combining signals using logic gates.

For example: Q = A AND B means "connect A and B into an AND gate, and the result is Q."

The letters are your inputs (A, B, C…) and Q is always the output.

Brackets = build this part first

Just like in maths, brackets tell you what to build first.

In Q = (A AND B) OR C, the brackets are around A AND B — so you build that AND gate before anything else.

You can't plug the AND gate's result into the OR gate until the AND gate exists — so always start inside the brackets!

What about NOT?

NOT appears right before an input or bracket, like NOT A or NOT (A AND B).

For NOT A: connect A straight into a NOT gate.

For NOT (A AND B): build the AND gate first (brackets first!), then connect its output into a NOT gate.

The rule is always: work from the inside out.

💡 Quick tip

Before placing any gates, count how many you need. Q = (A AND B) OR C needs one AND and one OR — place both first, then wire them up!
